## Design Context

### Users
This site serves multiple audiences at once: potential clients, internal team members,
recruiters, and community users. The interface needs to support both discovery and
confidence-building, helping people understand what each public agent does, how the
system is organized, and why the overall agency feels credible and differentiated.

### Brand Personality
The brand should feel cinematic, premium, and playful. It should project confidence
and polish without becoming sterile, and it should feel intentionally produced rather
than generically "AI-generated" or cheaply assembled.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction should evolve the current command-floor concept toward a more
premium editorial and enterprise-polished presentation. Keep the dramatic, immersive
energy of the agency world, but refine it with stronger hierarchy, cleaner composition,
more intentional spacing, and fewer cues that feel like a cheap futuristic template.

Anti-reference:
- cheap websites
- generic AI neon-dashboard aesthetics
- cluttered "tech demo" layouts that feel disposable

The experience must work well on both desktop and mobile, and it should meet WCAG AA
expectations while respecting reduced-motion preferences.

### Design Principles
1. Make the agency feel like a premium product, not a novelty landing page.
2. Use cinematic atmosphere sparingly and deliberately, with strong editorial hierarchy.
3. Keep the experience playful through motion, framing, and language, not gimmicks.
4. Preserve clarity and trust ahead of decorative effects.
5. Treat accessibility, responsive behavior, and reduced-motion support as part of quality.
